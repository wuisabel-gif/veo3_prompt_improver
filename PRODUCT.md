# PRODUCT.md — Real

## Register

**Brand.** The site is a public, design-forward showcase of a creative tool. The
landing/hero, prompt library, and "behind the scenes" pages sell the product;
the Studio Space tool is the product surface inside it. When they conflict,
distinctiveness wins for the marketing surfaces, plainness for the tool inputs.

## Product

**Real** — "make it real." A Veo 3 **fashion-film** prompt studio that turns a
rough idea into a structured, ultra-cinematic director's brief (editorials,
runway, beauty, luxury campaigns first; also short films, music videos,
visualizers). Ships as a web app plus a Gemini CLI extension, Chrome extension,
MCP server, and Claude skill, all driven by one shared prompt engine.

## Users & Purpose

- AI-video creators, fashion/editorial content makers, and prompt tinkerers who
  use Veo 3 / Gemini and want stronger, repeatable cinematic results.
- Their job: go from a vague idea to a copy-paste-ready Veo 3 brief in seconds,
  then hand it to a generator. Context: creative exploration, often at night,
  on desktop.
- Emotions to evoke: editorial confidence, cinematic taste, "this was made by
  someone with an eye." Premium but not corporate.

## Brand & Personality

Three words: **cinematic, editorial, nocturnal.** Dark gallery lighting, high
contrast, film-grade restraint with one expressive gradient accent
(cyan → indigo → pink). Think Vogue editorial meets a colorist's grading suite.

References that fit the feel: high-fashion campaign microsites (lots of negative
space, big confident type), a film color-grading UI (dark, precise), Arc/Linear-
grade polish on the tool controls.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing (cream bg, hero-metric template, identical icon-card grids).
- Crypto/neon overload — the gradient is an accent, not the whole surface.
- Stocky "AI app" clichés: glowing robots, purple-on-black bootstrap dashboards.

## Accessibility

- Dark theme; body text must hold ≥4.5:1 against near-black surfaces (watch the
  muted neutral-500 grays on dark — bump toward neutral-300/400 for real copy).
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any added motion.
- Keyboard-operable controls; visible focus states.

## Strategic principles

1. Show the craft: the output brief and the visual models are the hero, not chrome.
2. One accent system (the gradient) used sparingly for emphasis and brand, never
   as decoration on every element.
3. Editorial typography and generous negative space over dense card grids.
